Loan fee annualizer (simple)

Spread total upfront fees evenly over the loan term. The result is a simple yearly percentage for rough comparison only—it is not the APR on a federal Truth in Lending disclosure. For note rate + fees → APR (actuarial estimate), use the APR calculator.

How this calculation works

What each input means

  • Loan principal — Amount borrowed (denominator).
  • Total upfront fees — One-time charges you want to annualize (for example origination or points you group together). This tool does not split financed fees from cash fees.
  • Loan term (years) — Used only as a simple divisor to spread the fee load.

Formula used

Simple annualized fee rate (%) ≈ (fees ÷ principal ÷ years) × 100

This is a planning shortcut, not Truth-in-Lending APR: it ignores payment timing, compounding, nominal interest rate, and lender rounding rules.

How this result is estimated

This loan fee comparison tool provides estimate-level outputs based on the values you enter. Review assumptions and verify important decisions independently.

  • Inputs are user-provided and may include rounding.
  • Results are informational and not financial, tax, legal, or medical advice.
  • For high-impact decisions, confirm with a licensed professional.

Frequently asked questions

Is this the same APR as on my loan paperwork?

No. The APR on a Truth in Lending disclosure reflects your nominal rate, payment schedule, how finance charges and fees are applied over time, and other regulatory rules. This page only annualizes the total upfront fee dollars you enter, spread evenly by principal and years.

What does the percentage here mean?

It is (total upfront fees ÷ loan principal ÷ years in the loan) × 100, expressed as a simple percent per year. It is a rough fee load, not a full APR.

When should I use this?

For quick comparisons when you already have a ballpark for upfront costs and want a simple yearly figure. For borrowing decisions, rely on your lender’s official disclosures and qualified professionals.
