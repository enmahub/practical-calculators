How this calculation works

What each input means

  • First day of last period — Anchor date.
  • Cycle length (days) — Typical menstrual cycle length you enter.

Formula used

Adds (cycle length − 14) days to the last-period date to approximate ovulation (about two weeks before the next expected period). The page also shows a rough fertile window (about two days before through two days after that estimate).

Simplified calendar estimate—not medical timing or fertility treatment guidance.

How this result is estimated

This cycle-based ovulation estimator provides estimate-level outputs based on the values you enter. Review assumptions and verify important decisions independently.

  • Inputs are user-provided and may include rounding.
  • Results are informational and not financial, tax, legal, or medical advice.
  • For high-impact decisions, confirm with a licensed professional.

Frequently asked questions

Is this ovulation date medically exact?

No. It uses a simple calendar rule (about 14 days before your next expected period) and is for general planning only—not diagnosis, treatment, or contraception timing.

What is the fertile window shown here?

It is a rough range around the estimated ovulation date (about two days before through two days after). Real fertility varies by person and cycle.

Should I use this instead of talking to a clinician?

For health decisions, use professional guidance and not this calculator alone.
