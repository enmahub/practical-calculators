How this calculation works

What each input means

  • Start weight and goal weight — Use the same units for both (pounds, kilograms, etc.).

Formula used

Difference = start − goal. Positive means the goal is below the start (common “weight to lose” case); negative means the goal is above the start.

Total body-weight math only—not body fat, lean mass, timelines, or medical advice.

How this result is estimated

This weight-difference calculator applies the formulas described on this page to the values you enter. Outputs are not financial, tax, legal, or medical advice.

  • Inputs are user-provided and may include rounding.
  • Where accuracy, eligibility, or obligations matter, rely on official disclosures and licensed professionals.

Frequently asked questions

Does this measure body fat loss?

No. It only subtracts your goal weight from your start weight in the units you enter. It does not estimate fat mass, muscle change, or health risk.

What if my goal weight is higher than my start weight?

The result is a negative number (weight gain in the same units). The tool is a numeric difference only—not a diet or treatment plan.

Can I use this for medical decisions?

No. It is for quick illustration only. For nutrition or medical goals, consult a qualified professional.
