Water Intake Calculator

Estimates a daily fluid planning range from body weight plus simple bumps for activity and heat. It counts all fluids toward the total unless your clinician gave you a restriction—many people meet part of this through food. Kidney disease, heart failure, pregnancy, breastfeeding, or fluid limits mean you should follow medical guidance instead of online estimates.

How this calculation works

What each input means

  • Weight — Converted to kilograms internally.
  • Activity tier — Adds a flat millilitre bump for light/moderate/heavy usual days.
  • Exercise minutes — Adds ~350 ml per 30 minutes of extra sweating effort.
  • Climate — Optional extra bump for hot/high-sweat environments.

Formula used

Baseline ≈ 35 ml × body weight (kg) per day (widely used rough guideline). Total sums baseline + activity + exercise + heat adjustments, then shows ±10% as an individuality band.

Counts beverages generally—not “plain water only.” Ignore or adjust per clinician advice if you have fluid restrictions.

How this result is estimated

This daily water intake estimator applies the formulas described on this page to the values you enter. Outputs are not financial, tax, legal, or medical advice.

  • Inputs are user-provided and may include rounding.
  • Where accuracy, eligibility, or obligations matter, rely on official disclosures and licensed professionals.

Frequently asked questions

Do coffee or tea count toward hydration?

For most healthy adults, caffeinated beverages still contribute to fluids. If your clinician wants you counting only certain fluids, follow that instruction instead.

What if I have kidney or heart fluid limits?

Follow your medical team’s restriction. This calculator is not for clinically prescribed fluid ceilings.

Why show a percentage band around the estimate?

Needs swing with sweat, diet moisture, and individual thirst response; the band reflects that uncertainty—not precision.
