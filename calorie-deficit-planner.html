How this calculation works

What each input means

  • Maintenance calories / day — The energy budget you treat as “break even” (often from a TDEE-style estimate like the daily calorie calculator on this site).
  • Current and goal weight — Same units throughout (lb or kg).
  • Timeline — Either a fixed number of weeks or a goal date; the page counts whole days from today to that date (local calendar).

Formula used

Absolute mass change Δ = |current − goal|. Rule-of-thumb energy: ≈ 3,500 kcal per lb or ≈ 7,700 kcal per kg. Average daily deficit (loss) ≈ (Δ in lb × 3,500) ÷ days, or (Δ in kg × 7,700) ÷ days. Illustrative intake ≈ maintenance − average deficit (surplus for intentional weight gain).

Does not model metabolic adaptation, muscle gain, nutrient timing, or minimum safe intake—aggressive deficits deserve professional oversight.

How this result is estimated

This calorie deficit timeline planner applies the formulas described on this page to the values you enter. Outputs are not financial, tax, legal, or medical advice.

Frequently asked questions

Is this the same as the Daily Calorie Calculator?

No. The daily calorie tool estimates maintenance-style calories from demographics and activity. This planner starts from maintenance you supply (often from that page) and spreads a weight goal across weeks or a target date using rule-of-thumb energy density.

Why might my real weight change differ?

Metabolism adapts, muscle changes alter scale weight, sodium shifts water retention, and calorie labels are imperfect. The planner illustrates math from your inputs—not guaranteed biology.

Is a larger deficit always better?

No. Very low intakes can miss nutrients and raise medical risk. Discuss aggressive deficits with a qualified professional.
