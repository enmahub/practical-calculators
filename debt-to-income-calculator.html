Debt-to-Income (DTI) Calculator

Back-end DTI here means total minimum monthly debt payments ÷ gross monthly income, expressed as a percent ((debt ÷ income) × 100). Front-end ratios (housing-only vs income) and which debts belong in the numerator differ by lender and loan program. Related pages run separate arithmetic only: Mortgage calculator, Loan calculator, APR calculator.

Definitions (informational)

  • Front-end DTI — Some underwriting contexts compare housing-related payment to gross income; exact definitions vary.
  • Back-end DTI — Total recurring debt payments compared to gross income; included debts vary.
  • Figures in third-party sources — Articles and program materials sometimes reference percentages such as 28%, 36%, or 43%; they are not universal rules.

This page does not assess creditworthiness or eligibility. For requirements that affect you, refer to official loan/program disclosures or a qualified professional.

Arithmetic examples

  • $5,000 gross income, $1,200 debts → DTI ≈ 24%.
  • $6,500 gross income, $2,600 debts → DTI = 40%.

How this calculation works

What each input means

  • Monthly gross income — Denominator for the ratio.
  • Monthly debt payments — Total recurring minimum payments you choose to include (credit cards, auto, housing, other installment debt, etc.). Which debts belong in the ratio depends on the lender or program.

Formula used

DTI (%) ≈ (monthly debt ÷ monthly gross income) × 100 as implemented—a generic back-end-style ratio.

Front-end vs back-end: Some lenders emphasize housing payment vs income (“front-end”) separately from all debts (“back-end”). This page uses one combined debts field.

Lenders use underwriting-specific definitions; this is not a credit decision.
